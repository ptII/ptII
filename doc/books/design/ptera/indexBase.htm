Ptolemy II Ptera Demonstrations

    Ptera is an event-oriented model of computation for heterogeneous systems. This page collects examples and makes them available via webstart or as an applet. If you invoke the webstart version, then you can modify and save the model on your local disk. The applet version has no access to your local disk. The following examples are provided:
    • A simple model with two events.
    • A model with multiple events.
    • A model with arguments for the events and a canceling relation.
    • A model with simultaneous events.
    • A model that simulates a car wash system.
    • A hierarchical car wash.
    • A car wash model using DE, Ptera and FSM.
    • A model of an intersection with traffic lights.
    Ptera is described in more detail in the following technical memo:
    T. H. Feng, E. A. Lee, and L. W. Schruben, "Ptera: An Event-Oriented Model of Computation," EECS Department, University of California, Berkeley, Tech. Rep. UCB/EECS-2010-40, April 2010.

    Ptera is built in Ptolemy II, described in more detail at http://ptolemy.org.
